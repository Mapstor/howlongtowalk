# How Far Can You Walk in 15 Minutes?

**You can walk approximately 0.75 miles (1.2 km) in 15 minutes** at a moderate 3.0 mph pace. At a brisk 3.5 mph, you'll cover about 0.88 miles. At a leisurely 2.0 mph, roughly 0.5 miles. These estimates come from Bohannon & Andrews (2011), a meta-analysis of 23,111 subjects.

Fifteen minutes is the smallest practical walking time block. It's enough to reach anything within a 0.5–1.0 mile radius of wherever you are — making it the key distance for "walkability" decisions.

At fast walking pace, 15 minutes covers exactly 1 mile — a satisfying benchmark.

## What Can You Do with a 15-Minute Walk?

Fifteen minutes is the "walk or drive" decision threshold. Here's what fits within that time:

**Steps:** Approximately 1,689 steps at moderate pace (2,252 steps/mile × 0.75 miles, per *ACSM's Health & Fitness Journal*, 2008).

**Calories:** About 40–53 calories for a 150–200 lb person (body weight × 0.53 × 0.75 miles).

**Health value:** Two 15-minute walks per day provide 30 minutes of moderate activity — 21% of the CDC's recommended 150 minutes per week.

**Practical radius:** Everything within 0.75 miles of your home, office, or hotel is accessible in a 15-minute walk. Map your 15-minute radius and you'll be surprised how much falls within it.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is a 15-minute walk enough exercise?

A single 15-minute walk is a meaningful contribution to daily activity. It adds about 1,689 steps and 40–53 calories. Done twice daily, it provides 30 minutes of moderate exercise — one-fifth of the CDC's weekly recommendation.

Fifteen minutes is also the minimum duration shown to improve mood and mental clarity in multiple studies.

### How many steps is a 15-minute walk?

Approximately **1,689 steps** at moderate pace. At brisk pace (~100 steps per minute per *British Journal of Sports Medicine*, 2018), you'd log about 1,500 steps in 15 minutes.
