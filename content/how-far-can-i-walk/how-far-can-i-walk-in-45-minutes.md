# How Far Can You Walk in 45 Minutes?

**You can walk approximately 2.25 miles (3.6 km) in 45 minutes** at a moderate 3.0 mph pace. At a brisk 3.5 mph, you'll cover about 2.63 miles. At a leisurely 2.0 mph, roughly 1.5 miles. These estimates come from Bohannon & Andrews (2011), a meta-analysis of 23,111 subjects.

Forty-five minutes is the sweet spot between "quick walk" and "dedicated workout." At brisk pace, you'll cover nearly 5K (3.1 miles) — making a 45-minute brisk walk essentially a walking 5K.

At fast pace, 45 minutes covers exactly 3 miles — a clean fitness walking benchmark.

## What Can You Do with a 45-Minute Walk?

**Steps:** Approximately 5,067 steps at moderate pace (2,252 × 2.25 miles).

**Calories:** About 119–159 calories for a 150–200 lb person (body weight × 0.53 × 2.25 miles).

**Health value:** A daily 45-minute walk provides 315 minutes of activity per week — more than double the CDC's 150-minute recommendation. Even three 45-minute walks per week (135 minutes) nearly meets the guideline.

**Fitness equivalent:** At brisk pace, a 45-minute walk covers nearly 5K distance. Three brisk 45-minute walks per week is essentially a 5K walking habit — delivering real cardiovascular and metabolic benefits.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is a 45-minute walk a good workout?

A 45-minute walk is an excellent workout. At moderate pace, it burns 119–159 calories, covers over 2 miles, and adds 5,000+ steps to your day. At brisk pace, it qualifies as moderate-intensity exercise per CDC guidelines and covers nearly 5K — a meaningful fitness distance.

Done daily, a 45-minute walk exceeds every public health activity recommendation.

### How many steps is a 45-minute walk?

Approximately **5,067 steps** at moderate pace (3.0 mph). At brisk pace (~100 steps/minute per *British Journal of Sports Medicine*, 2018), about 4,500 steps. Either way, a 45-minute walk gets you past the halfway point toward 10,000 daily steps.
