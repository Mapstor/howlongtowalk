# How Long Does It Take to Run a Half Marathon?

**The average half marathon finish time is approximately 2 hours and 1 minute**, based on RunRepeat (2024, 35 million results). Men average about 1 hour 55 minutes and women about 2 hours 11 minutes. A half marathon is 13.109 miles (21.097 km) — the most popular long-distance race in the world, with over 2 million US finishers annually.

The half marathon is often called "the sweet spot" of distance running. It's long enough to feel like a genuine endurance achievement, short enough that most people can train for it in 10–14 weeks without upending their lives.

A **sub-2-hour half marathon** (9:09/mile) is the signature goal for recreational runners. A **sub-1:45** (8:00/mile) indicates strong recreational fitness. A **sub-1:30** (6:52/mile) is advanced.

The men's world record is 57:31 (Jacob Kiplimo, 2021). The women's is 1:02:52 (Letesenbet Gidey, 2021).

## 5 Real-World Examples

### 1. The Sub-2 First-Timer

Alex, 31, trains 12 weeks targeting sub-2:00. He runs four days per week, building his long run from 8 to 12 miles. Strategy: start at 9:00/mile, hold steady. Finish: **1:57:34**.

His 1:57 places him in the top 40% of men. At 170 lbs, he burns approximately **1,672 calories** (170 × 0.75 × 13.1). The sub-2 finish — with 2:26 to spare — feels like the biggest athletic achievement of his life.

### 2. The Beginner Run-Walker

Priya, 44, uses a run-walk strategy: run 3 min, walk 1 min, repeat. Her average pace works out to 11:15/mile. Finish: **2:27:30**. She never bonks, never walks longer than planned, and finishes feeling strong.

Her 2:27 is near the bottom 25% by time — but she's ahead of everyone who didn't show up. The run-walk strategy is increasingly endorsed by coaches for first-timers.

### 3. The PR Chaser

Vanessa, 36, has four halfs (PR: 1:48) and wants sub-1:45. She adds weekly tempo runs at 7:45/mile and long runs with the final 3 miles at goal pace. Finish: **1:43:22** — a 5-minute PR.

Her 1:43 places her in the top 10% of all women (RunRepeat: under 1:50). The improvement came from disciplined pacing — 7:55 for the first 10 miles, 7:50 for the final 5K.

### 4. The Social Runner

Tom, 55, runs a half every year with his college friends — their annual reunion race. He trains casually (3 runs/week, 15–20 miles total) and finishes in about **2:10** (9:56/mile). He's not chasing time — he's chasing the post-race beer.

His 2:10 is near the male average and well above average for 55–59. He's run the same event 8 times (1:58 to 2:15 range).

### 5. The 68-Year-Old Competitor

Joan, 68, has run halfs for 15 years. Her current **2:28** (11:18/mile) earns consistent age-group placements. Her PR was 1:52 at age 55 — a 36-minute decline over 13 years, roughly 2–3 minutes per year.

She trains three days per week with one long run of 8–10 miles. Her 2:28 still places her well above average for all women.

## How to Train for a Half Marathon

**Build over 10–14 weeks.** Your long run should reach 10–12 miles. You don't need to run 13.1 in training — race-day adrenaline and taper carry the final miles.

**Run 4 days per week minimum.** Two easy runs, one speed or tempo session, one long run. Weekly mileage peaks at 25–35 miles for most recreational runners.

**Run the first 5 miles at controlled effort.** The biggest half marathon mistake is going out too fast. Run the first 5K at 10–15 seconds per mile slower than goal pace. Restraint early creates strength late.

**Take gels after mile 7.** A half marathon is long enough to benefit from mid-race fueling, especially if you're out there longer than 1:45. Practice in training.

**Negative split if possible.** Running the second half faster than the first is smart pacing. Aim to feel "too slow" at mile 3 and "just right" at mile 10.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is a good half marathon time?

Under 2:00 is a strong recreational benchmark. Under 1:45 indicates dedicated training. Under 1:30 is advanced. Simply finishing 13.1 miles is something most people never attempt.

Among women, under 1:50 is top 10%. Among men, under 1:37 is top 10% (RunRepeat, 2024).

### How does a half marathon predict marathon time?

Multiply by roughly 2.1. A 1:45 half: ~3:40–3:50 marathon. A 2:00 half: ~4:10–4:20. The half marathon is the single best predictor of marathon potential because it tests endurance at a similar effort level.

### Can beginners run a half marathon?

Yes, with proper training. Most beginner plans are 10–14 weeks, require a base of running 3–4 miles comfortably, and build to a peak long run of 10–12 miles. Run-walk strategies are completely legitimate.

The half marathon is the most popular US race distance for a reason — it's challenging but achievable for nearly anyone willing to train.

### What is the hardest part of a half marathon?

Miles 10–11. By this point you've been running for 80–100 minutes and the finish line still feels distant. The final 2 miles are often mentally easier because the end is visible. Strategy: break miles 10–11 into half-mile chunks. Don't think about the remaining distance.
