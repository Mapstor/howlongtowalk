# How Long Does It Take to Walk 2 km?

**It takes approximately 25 minutes to walk 2 km (1.24 miles)** at an average walking pace of 4.8 km/h (3.0 mph). At a brisk pace of 5.6 km/h (3.5 mph), you'll finish in about 21 minutes and 26 seconds, while a leisurely walker at 3.2 km/h (2.0 mph) may need 37 minutes 30 seconds. These estimates come from Bohannon & Andrews (2011), a meta-analysis of 23,111 subjects.

**Common 2 km walks:** The walk from a suburban bus stop to home, a stroll between two nearby shops, a loop around a medium-sized park, or a child's walk to school. Two kilometres is the distance that makes walking feel natural rather than like a commute.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is walking 2 km a day enough exercise?

Walking 2 km daily provides about 175 minutes of moderate activity per week (25 min × 7 days) — exceeding the CDC's recommended 150 minutes. It adds roughly 2,793 steps per walk, meaningfully boosting daily activity for sedentary adults.

For maximum health benefit, walking 2 km is a strong minimum. Gradually increasing to 3–5 km amplifies the benefits.

### How many steps is 2 km?

Approximately **2,793 steps** at moderate pace. By height: taller people take fewer steps (about 2,400 at 193 cm / 6'4") and shorter people take more (about 3,100 at 152 cm / 5'0").

### How does walking 2 km compare to running it?

Running 2 km at a recreational pace of 6:00/km takes about 12 minutes. Walking at moderate pace takes 25 minutes — about twice as long. Running burns roughly 40% more calories per kilometre.
